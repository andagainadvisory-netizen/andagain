Ethiopian exporters and the UAE: your biggest market, barely tapped

The UAE is already the number one destination for Ethiopian exports. Yet most of that trade is raw, sold through middlemen, and far below its potential. Here is how to change that.

14 min read 2026By And Again Advisory

If you are an Ethiopian exporter, your most important international customer is closer than you think. It is not in Europe, Asia, or North America. It is four hours away by plane, across the Red Sea, in the United Arab Emirates.

The UAE is already Ethiopia's single largest export destination. Ethiopian coffee, sesame, pulses, flowers, gold, and more flow into Dubai and Abu Dhabi every week. And yet the relationship is running well below its potential. Most of what Ethiopia sends leaves as raw commodity, priced by others, sold through layers of intermediaries, and rarely carrying an Ethiopian brand. The value that could stay with Ethiopian exporters is being left on the table.

This guide is an invitation to look again at the market on your doorstep: what you can sell, why the UAE is the natural place to sell it, the value you have not captured yet, and how And Again Advisory helps you set up in the UAE and connect directly with buyers.

01Why the UAE is your natural market

The numbers make the case on their own. In recent trade data the UAE takes around 18 percent of Ethiopia's goods exports, ahead of every other country, and much of the rest of the world's trade with Ethiopia is routed through Dubai as an intermediary platform because of its proximity and reliable air links (US Department of Commerce). This is happening while Ethiopia's exports are booming, with export earnings reaching USD 2.48 billion in a single quarter of 2025, up around 117 percent year on year, against a national target of USD 9.4 billion (Businessfront).

Beyond the statistics, the fundamentals are compelling:

  • Proximity and logistics. A short flight and quick sea routes mean fresh produce, flowers, and perishables arrive fast, and freight costs stay low.
  • A hungry, diverse market. Millions of residents from every corner of the world, plus a large Ethiopian and East African community, create ready demand for coffee, food, and commodities.
  • A trusted trading environment. World-class ports, transparent customs, strong banking, and enforceable contracts make the UAE a safe place to get paid.
  • A halal and premium food hub. The UAE is a global centre for halal trade and premium food retail, a natural fit for Ethiopian agricultural products.
  • A springboard. Selling in the UAE also opens the door to the wider Gulf, South Asia, and Africa through re-export.

Your biggest export customer is already the UAE. The question is how much more of that value you keep.

02What Ethiopia can export

Ethiopia's export strengths line up almost perfectly with UAE demand. Coffee alone makes up more than a quarter of Ethiopia's exports, followed by gold, oilseeds, vegetables, flowers, and pulses (US Department of Commerce). Here is where the opportunity is richest:

CoffeeThe birthplace of coffee, and the UAE's specialty scene is booming. Huge room for branded, roasted, single-origin Ethiopian coffee.
Oilseeds and sesameEthiopian sesame is prized across the Gulf and Asia. The UAE is a major trading and re-export point for it.
Pulses and legumesChickpeas, lentils, and beans feed the region's large South Asian and Arab populations year round.
Cut flowersEthiopia is a top global rose grower. Fast air freight makes the UAE and its onward markets ideal.
Gold and mineralsGold is a leading Ethiopian export, and Dubai is the world's second largest gold hub.
Leather and goodsEthiopian hides are world class. Finished leather goods capture far more value than raw hides.
Honey and spicesDistinctive Ethiopian honey, berbere, and spices suit the UAE's premium and specialty grocery shelves.
Fruit and vegetablesAvocados, herbs, and fresh produce meet strong, steady demand from UAE retail and hospitality.
Meat and livestockHalal meat and livestock are long-standing exports with consistent Gulf demand.

The pattern is clear. Ethiopia produces what the UAE and its onward markets want to buy. The gap is not demand. It is how the trade is structured.

03The value you have not tapped yet

Here is the honest part. Most Ethiopian exports leave the country as raw or barely processed commodities, sold to intermediaries who capture the margin that comes from processing, branding, and owning the customer relationship. A sack of green coffee or raw sesame earns a fraction of what the same product earns once it is roasted, packaged, branded, and placed on a UAE shelf.

Raw today

  • Green coffee sold in bulk
  • Unbranded sesame and pulses
  • Raw hides and skins
  • Sold through intermediaries
  • Price set by others

Value added tomorrow

  • Roasted, branded coffee on UAE shelves
  • Packaged, labelled sesame and pulses
  • Finished leather goods
  • Direct buyer and retail relationships
  • You set the price and own the brand

The second untapped lever is direct access. When you sell through a chain of middlemen, you never meet the buyer, never learn what the market wants, and never build a brand. Setting up a presence in the UAE lets you sell directly to importers, distributors, retailers, roasters, and the hospitality sector, and to keep the relationship and the data that come with it.

The opportunity is not to export more sacks. It is to export brands, relationships, and finished value.

04The UAE as a gateway, not just a market

Selling in the UAE is valuable on its own. But the bigger prize is what the UAE unlocks. Dubai is one of the world's great re-export hubs, and a huge share of trade with Ethiopia already moves through it to reach other markets. Establish a base in the UAE and you are not selling to one country of ten million, you are positioned to reach a market of more than two billion consumers across the Gulf, South Asia, wider Africa, and beyond.

An Ethiopian exporter with a UAE company can consolidate shipments, re-export to onward buyers, hold stock in bonded warehouses, and invoice and get paid in a stable, convertible environment. The UAE becomes your international sales and distribution arm, close to home and open to the world.

05What it takes to sell into the UAE

Entering the market properly means a few building blocks, none of them out of reach with the right support:

  • A UAE trade licence. A company with the right trading or foodstuff activity, on the mainland or in a free zone, gives you a legal base to import, sell, and re-export.
  • Product and food-safety compliance. Food and agricultural goods must meet UAE standards, labelling, and, where relevant, halal and municipality requirements.
  • Logistics and customs. A customs code, freight and cold-chain arrangements, and often a bonded or free zone facility for re-export.
  • Banking and payment. A corporate bank account so you can invoice buyers and receive payment securely and transparently.
  • Buyers and channels. The relationships that turn a licence into actual sales, which is where most exporters need the most help.

Frequently asked questions

Is the UAE really a good market for Ethiopian exporters?

Yes. The UAE is already Ethiopia's single largest export destination, and Dubai is widely used as a re-export platform to the wider region. Proximity, fast logistics, a large expatriate population, and strong food and commodity demand make it a natural first international market.

What can Ethiopia export to the UAE?

Coffee, oilseeds such as sesame, pulses and legumes, cut flowers, gold, leather and leather goods, spices, honey, fruit and vegetables, meat and livestock, and processed and packaged foods. The largest opportunity is usually in adding value rather than exporting raw commodities alone.

What is the untapped opportunity?

Most Ethiopian exports leave as raw commodities sold through intermediaries. The untapped value is in processing and branding, reaching UAE buyers directly, and using the UAE as a base to re-export to onward markets.

Do I need to be based in the UAE to export there?
